Modeling Feshbach Resonances in Ultracold Atoms
2010 - 2014
Tags: atomic physics, physical modeling, computational physics

Computationally modeling behaviors of atoms near absolute zero.
With high power lasers, we created a small potential well in which ultracold atoms will be trapped. These atoms are trapped due to rather low kinetic energy they possess (intuitively speaking, they're sluggish 'cause they're almost frozen). And near the top boundaries of the potential energy well, Feshbach resonances occur. I studied how to model atoms at that boundary for my undergraduate honors thesis.
